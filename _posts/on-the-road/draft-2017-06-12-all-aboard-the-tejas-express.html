We are making our way from Goa to Mumbai, and find ourselves once again at the Carambolim Railway Station, better known to the visiting masses as Karmali. I think Karmali took precedence over Carambolim, as the trailing "m" normally leads to the butchering of names in Goa. A few examples are Candolim, Siolim, Cortalim, Borim, the list goes on.

There some then thousand people at the platorm! This tiny railway station normally has a moving population of 17. That includes the stay dog taking a random piss on the tracks. Unfortunately school did not start off early, and we are met with a bunch of vacationers making their way back to their respective cities with bright lights and air that makes my uncle's 20-year old Bullet exhaust look good. Anyway, all it takes is a few steps away from the junta and we find ourselves with a lot of space to spead our arms. I put on my CSI glasses and realize that the doors of the train are closed. Hence the masses were strewn around on the platform. We get to our coach #7, which luckily did not have to may people around.

First impression – The train looks really good. The huge windows are definitly a way forward. This country is finally showing signs of progress. Luckily the junta did rush to the doors like they normally do. All that education is finally start to kick in I guess. I should've carried my box of medals and handed it out to all these people. They almost showed signs of forming a queue, suddenly some signs of patriotism emerged and a bunch of folks decided to get to ahead of themselves by forming a queue, and decided to push from all sides. Where are those medals when you need them.

We found our seats easily. 77 and 78. Right next to the door. The train left bang on 1430. If I'm not mistaken, it was supossed to depart at 1435. Maybe my watch was not calibrated to the position of the sun. I think the monsoon clouds might have got in the way of my sundial. Anyway, earlier is better than late.

A couple of kilometers in the trip and the train was flying, like as though all those years of piss and faeces hitting the tracks had finally come to some use. This one was moving at a really fast pace, unlike the slow coaches we've taken in the past. IST 14:45 and we have just crossed Thivim. This eagle is totally flying.

I'm not sure why we've got the corner most seats, but I guess it is because these are one of the two seater rows in the four cornsers of the coach. I'm assuming the booking algorithm knew we were a couple and did not want some clown seating with us.

Now getting to trains, I think I might know a thing or two about this complicated network of metal monstors pumping their way around this huge subcontinent. From sleeping in the cool Rajdhani to Delhi, to standing in the general compartments with potential murdereres from a certain North Indian state, I've might have traveressed a huge part of this country using the Indian Railways. So when we realized that the Tejas express was running from a station right next door to home, we cancelled our tickets on the stupid Konkan Kanya and booked seats on the Tejas Express. Now I take my train journeys seriously alright and this journey was no joke for me. Let's now look at what the Tejas has to offer.

Ergonomics

I'm assuming that the person incharge of ergonomics might have never sat in a train in his or her entire life.

If you want to use a laptop, then I suggest your use it after you alight the train. Firstly laptops don's fit properly on the tray in front. You can't have the sreen opened up properly, and if the person in front of you decided to pus his seat back, then be prepared to say "Thank you Sir. Alleluiah, amen!", as you entire tray will move back and basically there is not point to life after this. Let's not get too excited about this, as I've not had any expectations from the ergonomics department.

The arm rests don't fold up. Which mean only one thing. People with a lot of fat deposits on their upper thighs are going to have a tough time squeezing into their seats. This also means less physical contact with the missus who is sitting next to me. Our midriffs wont get to touch for sure. After a few tries we realized that the arm rests did move up. It just happens that you need a gym membership to get it to work

The sliding doors are horribly designed. Pretty tough for the older folks and kids to handle. It seems like they've not learnt anything from those gullotine doors they had provided on the double decker train earlier. A few kids almost getting their skulls smashed in (which would be nice for the population issue we have), and older folks just did not know what to do when they reached the doors.

Comfort

The seats are quite comfortable and push a long way back. The lower back support seems to be okay, but there is someting off about it at the same time. I think I might have accumulated some extra fat on the back side. I'll give the Indian Railways the benefit of doubt on this one. The seats are also extremely high.

Storage space also gets extra points. The overhead storage racks were pretty spacious, and there was enough space to shove a small bag under your seat. There was space to store a coupld of bigger suitcases behind our (last) seats, and the aisles were pretty spacious too.

The air conditioning is maintained at a pretty livable temperature so far. The quest to transport frozen bodies must only be a thing on the double decker trains. 14:55 and we are in Pernem!
